## Session: 2026-01-04 — Concept Exploration

### Context
First session. Starting from a vague hunch ("podcast about bread") and trying to figure out whether there's actually something here, or whether this is just one of fifty bread podcasts already on the market.

### Key Thinking

**Why this, why now**

The home baking surge from 2020 produced a generation of intermediate bakers — people who have made dozens of loaves, watched all the YouTube videos, and now want something deeper. The existing podcast field is split into two extremes: produced-for-beginners (everything explained from scratch, glacial pacing) and inside-baseball professional content (bakery economics, lamination technique for laminated dough rooms with steam ovens). Nothing in the middle for the home baker who already knows what a banneton is but wants to understand why their crumb keeps coming out tight.

**The interesting tension**

Pros and home bakers approach bread differently in ways neither side fully sees. Pros optimize for consistency at volume. Home bakers optimize for the specific loaf in front of them, often using equipment pros would consider absurd. When they talk, they argue. The arguments are the interesting part.

**Format ideas explored**

- Solo show, host explains topics — boring, exists already
- Interview format, host + one guest per episode — generic, exists already
- **Two-guest argument format** — pair one pro with one serious home baker, give them a topic, let them argue. Host moderates lightly. This is the format that doesn't exist and seems worth trying.

The two-guest format has the strongest pull but also the most production risk. Bookings get harder. Audio quality from three sources. Conversations could go flat if guests don't actually disagree.

### Decisions Made

**F1 — Format is the two-guest argument structure.** One professional baker, one serious home baker, one topic per episode. Host moderates, asks clarifying questions, keeps things on track. Not finalizing duration yet.

**F2 — Target audience: intermediate home bakers.** People who have made bread before, want to get better, are not looking for beginner explainers. This excludes both pure novices and working professionals.

### Open Questions

- **F-OQ1:** Episode duration. Argument format implies longer (30-60 min) but attention-span data suggests 20-30 min cap for indie podcasts.
- **F-OQ2:** Cadence. Weekly is the default, but is it sustainable with two-guest bookings?
- **F-OQ3:** Solo episodes between guest episodes? Tempting for filler but might dilute the core promise.

## Session: 2026-01-08 — Episode Duration and Cadence

### Context
Pick up from F-OQ1 (duration) and F-OQ2 (cadence). Goal: lock in the answers so we can start booking pilot episodes.

### Key Thinking

**Duration data**

Pulled benchmarks across indie podcasts in the food/craft space:
- 99% Invisible: 25-30 min, consistently. Format is tight and narrative-driven.
- Sporkful: 35-45 min, variable. Format is loose and interview-driven.
- Heritage Radio Network shows: 30-60 min. Often padded.

The 35-45 min range felt baggy in our spot-checks. The 25-30 min range felt deliberate. The padding in longer shows mostly came from intros and ads.

**Argument format implications**

A genuine argument needs setup, exchange, and resolution. Setup takes ~3 min (topic introduction, guest credentials). Resolution takes ~3 min (what each guest will take away). That leaves 19-24 min for the actual exchange in a 25-30 min show. Felt tight but workable on a whiteboarding pass.

**Cadence**

Weekly is unsustainable with two-guest bookings. Each episode needs 2-3 weeks of lead time for booking + recording. Bi-weekly is the right cadence for a one-person operation. Monthly feels too slow to build audience momentum.

**Filler episodes**

Tempting to fill the off-weeks with solo episodes (host explaining a technique, reading listener mail, etc.) but every example we looked at showed dilution: the solo episodes underperformed and the format started to feel like "any podcast about bread." The core promise is the two-guest argument. Better to publish less and keep the promise pure.

### Decisions Made

**F3 — Episode duration: 25 min, edited tight.** Closer to "deliberate" than "baggy." If a conversation goes 60 min in the room, cut it ruthlessly to 25.

**F4 — Cadence: bi-weekly.** Sustainable with one host, two guests per episode, 2-3 week lead time.

**F5 — No filler episodes.** Two-guest argument only. Off-weeks stay off-weeks.

### Open Questions

- **F-OQ4:** What is the first season's topic list? Need 4-6 strong topics that produce real disagreement, not just curiosity. (Closes F-OQ3 from previous session by negation — no filler episodes means no need to plan around them.)
