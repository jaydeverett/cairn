# Process Meta Notes

> Observations about the **workflow itself**, not the project content. What worked, what was friction, what would make sessions better. Append-only, dated entries.
>
> Not every session produces observations. Only add entries when something genuinely notable happened. Empty weeks are fine — better silence than noise.
>
> Three categories: **What Worked**, **Friction Points**, **Product Insights** (lessons that could help others using cairn, not just you). Use whichever apply per session. Drop the ones that don't.

## 2026-01-08 — Session 2 (Episode Duration and Cadence)

### What Worked
- **Benchmarking before deciding.** Pulling concrete duration data from three reference podcasts before answering F-OQ1 grounded the decision in evidence rather than instinct. The 35-45 min range "felt baggy" was a vibe — the breakdown of where the baggy minutes came from (intros, ads, lack of editing) made it actionable.
- **Closing related open questions by negation.** Deciding "no filler episodes" (F5) implicitly closed F-OQ3 (whether to use filler). Naming this explicitly in "Changes to Master" prevented the question from lingering as a phantom open item.

### Friction Points
- **Open questions are easier to add than to close.** Five OQs opened across two sessions; only three closed. The brainstorm shows a clear pattern of new questions surfacing faster than old ones resolve. Worth periodically reviewing the OQ list and deciding which are still load-bearing versus quietly stale.

### Product Insights
- **The "RESUME HERE" line at the bottom of every brainstorm is doing more work than expected.** Pasting it into a new session as the cold-start prompt removes ~5 minutes of reorientation per session. The next-session-prompt deliverable in the workflow is the small habit that compounds the most.

## How to write a meta note (delete this section once you have your own entries)

A good entry is:

- **Specific.** "Benchmarking before deciding" beats "research helped." The reader should know what to imitate.
- **Honest about cost.** Friction points should describe what was annoying, not just what could be improved.
- **Optional.** Skip sessions where nothing notable happened. The point is the signal, not the volume.

Categories:

- **What Worked** — a pattern or habit that produced a better outcome than the default.
- **Friction Points** — something that wasted time, broke flow, or quietly degraded the work. Worth fixing or working around.
- **Product Insights** — lessons that would help someone else using cairn for a different project. These are the entries worth sharing externally if you want to contribute back to the template.

If you want to contribute interesting meta notes back to the cairn template, open a GitHub Issue or PR on the cairn repo and paste the entry. Useful patterns get folded into the README over time. **Share processes, never content** — keep your project's actual decisions out of the contribution; only share the workflow lesson.
